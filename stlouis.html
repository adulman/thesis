ST. LOUIS, MISSOURI

where the people are nice and the food is better

WEEKEND ITINERARY

11AM HALF & HALF

Half & Half is a hugely popular brunch spot. It's a cute place on a cute street in Clayton, the business district of St. Louis. Get the clara pancakes or the veggie hash, and try some of their fancy pour-over coffee.

Whoever said brussels sprouts couldn't be breakfast was seriously disturbed

12PM THE DELMAR LOOP

The Loop is a street called Delmar Blvd, nicknamed the Loop because of a trolley that loops up and down it. It has an eclectic mix of stores, offices, restaurants, and bars. Go to the thrift store Avalon if you wanna feel trendy, or Pi Pizzeria to try Obama's favorite pizza. Watch out for the homeless ppl.

1:30PM FOREST PARK

Forest Park is twice as big as Central Park in NYC, which St. Louis residents never shut up about. But it is pretty cool. It has an art musuem, a history museum, a golf course, a boathouse, a zoo, an ice skating rink, sports fields, a theatre, and more. The zoo is free if you wanna check it out, or you can rent a paddleboat, or just hang on Art Hill.

Being outdoorsy in a way that isn't getting drunk on a patio

2:30PM TED DREWES

Teddy's, as I like to call it, is a St. Louis staple. It's frozen custard concretes. Basically they blend ice cream with any candy or sauces you want to make it into this new incredible thing. Examples: mint & oreo, caramel & brownie, marshmallow & graham cracker & chocolate, banana & peanut butter... you can go wild. This shit is thiiick though and they prove it to you by flipping your concrete upside down before handing it to you.

3:00PM CHEROKEE STREET

Cherokee Street is a funky and cool street in STL. It's v artsy - there are galleries, design studios, and public art exhibitions, but also normal restaurants and stores.

Broken doors and brick walls are like so chic

4:30PM ANHEUSER-BUSCH

STL is the home of Budweiser... it doesn't get more midwestern than a city obsessed with beer. You can visit the brewery, meet the Clydesdales, and enjoy some beer in the Biergarten. Cheers!

7:30PM PASTARIA

A classically basic spot in STL. It's usually a scene but that adds to its amazingness. Pastaria is casual but nice, and has unreal pastas and pizzas. Start with risotto balls and don't skip the side of brussels. Finish with a taste of gelato from the case.

Carbe diem

10PM LOOP BARS

Head back to the Loop for a little bar crawl. Start at Blueberry Hill, which has a ton of cool STL history. Then head to Three Kings, a pubby student favorite. Next Moonrise, on the roof of the Moonrise Hotel, and finally Pin Up, a bowling alley bar.Honestly avoid the homeless people even more now that it's dark. STL can be scary.
