BARCELONA, SPAIN

where the party don't stop. ever.

WEEKEND ITINERARY

10AM LAS RAMBLAS

Las Ramblas is a main drag of Barcelona. There are usually a lot of really good (and really annoying) street performers.

10:30AM LA BOQUERIA

La Boqueria is a marketplace is right on Las Ramblas. It has tons of fresh fruits, juices, smoothies - great for breakfast. There's also pretty much anything else you want like nuts, candy, empanadas, and raw meat and fish yum!!

That good good

11:30AM GOTHIC QUARTER

The Barrio Gótico is the old, historical neighborhood of Barcelona and it's beautiful. You can walk around or do a segway tour - highly recommend this tbh it was so touristy but so great.

Even the old parts of Barc know how to party

1PM BO DE B

Bo de B is a lunch place with huge lines, but for a good reason. They have amazing, cheap sandwiches. Get jamón because you're in Spain.

2:30PM LA BARCELONETA

This is the beach area that you saw last night, but you were drunk so that doesn't count. Check out Port Vell and just walk along the boardwalk and take in the views.

4PM CDLC

CDLC means Carpe Diem Lounge Club which is probably the greatest name ever. It's a club at night, and a restaurant, but most importantly it's a great place to day drink. You can lay out on the couch beds and drink as the sun sets.

Happiness is sunglasses & sangria

7:30PM LA LUNA

A greattttt great restaurant. They have a tapas menu and a normal menu, but make sure you ask for the tapas one because the food is sick.

9:30PM LA XOCOLATERIA

The food doesn't stop. You can't leave Barc without having churros dipped in chocolate. It's mandatory.

When Europe interprets hot chocolate as literally melted chocolate >>>

1AM OPIUM

Probably the most famous of all the beach clubs. Opium is unreal - crazy music and lights, and it's pretty big. Don't lose your friends or your phone but definitely lose your mind.
