WEEKEND ITINERARY

12PM SLIM GOODIES

Slim Goodies is a casual diner on Magazine Street. It's ca$h only so be prepared. No matter what you order, make sure to get a fluffy sweet potato pancake on the side.

1PM MAGAZINE STREET

Magazine Street is a great strip in NOLA. It's six miles long and has everything from restaurants and coffee shops to bars and clubs to antique stores, art galleries, and craft shops. It feels v main street cute and charming. Pop into stores or chill at a sidewalk table of a café because it will be warm no matter when you're in New Orleans.

3PM HANSEN'S SNO-BLIZ

Hansen's is a NOLA institution. They have the best snow cones in like 20 different fruity or sweet flavors. It's tradition to use condensed milk as a topping - just roll with it, you're in the big easy. The place may look janky and run down, but you gotta trust the line that's two miles long.

Ice ice baby

4PM THE FLY

The Fly is an area of Audubon Park right on the banks of the Mississippi. People go here to picnic, day drink, tan, and relax. Bring a blanket, and settle in for the sunset.

Current status

7PM ATCHAFALAYA

Atchafalaya is more famous for brunch, but its dinner is just as good. The menu has Louisiana influences aka gumbo, grits, and lots of seafood, but there's more normal stuff too.

10PM F&M PATIO BAR

F&M's is a crowded college scene where you dance on pool tables and then black out getting their famous cheese fries. It's prob unacceptable to be here if you're older than 25.
