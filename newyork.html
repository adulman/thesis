WEEKEND ITINERARY

friday

12PM SHAKE SHACK

Hit up the original location of Shake Shack... legit a shack in Madison Square Park. Get a burger, fries, and a shake. Don't ask what is in the special shack sauce, just enjoy it in awe and wonder.

I'd shack up with you any night ;)

1PM THE HIGH LINE

The High Line is a sick park built on old elevated railroad tracks. Walk down the whole thing - there's green spaces, public art, food stands, lounge chairs, and great views.

2PM THE WHITNEY

The Whitney Museum of American Art was recently re-done and is now a necessary stop on the basic tour of NY. It's right below the High Line at 13th and Gansevoort, in the heart of Meatpacking, one of the trendiest neighborhoods in Manhattan. Not only is the art instagrammable, but there's also outdoor areas with cool colored chairs and city views.

Nothing like pretending to admire art so you can get an artsy insta!

3:30PM CHELSEA MARKET

Chelsea Market is right in Meatpacking and a great collective of shops and restaurants. Some of the most famous include Bar Suzette (crepes), Doughnuttery (mini donuts), Fat Witch Bakery, Hale & Hearty (a New York classic with soups, salads, and sandwiches), Num Pang (Vietnamese sandwiches), and Takumi (Japanese Mexican combo). Don't miss the Artist & Fleas market in the back, which is a trendy flea market with tons of art, jewelry, and fun trinkets.

4:30PM LOOPY DOOPY ROOFTOP BAR

Loopy Doopy is definitely as basic as it gets. This rooftop bar in the Financial District (FiDi) serves popsicles in glasses of wine. Go for sunset and make friends with all the other basic bitches there.

Loopy doopy doopity do, I've got a basic picture for you!

7:30PM DOS CAMINOS

A trendy Mexican restaurant with a few locations in the city. Good guac, good drinks, good food, good vibes.

11PM LE POISSON ROUGE

LPR is right next to Dos Caminos, and they have 90s night on Fridays hosted by a band called The Fresh Kids of Bel-Air. Sign me up.

1AM RIFF RAFF'S

After LPR, make your way to Riff Raff's, a smaller, more exclusive club. It's tiki themed, and the bartenders will put stripes of paint on your face or arms if you ask them to. Did you really get into Riff Raff's if you don't post a pic with your face painted? Answer: no, loser.

saturday

9:30AM TWO HANDS CAFE

Two Hands is a classic brunch spot, most famous for their avo toast and acai bowls. Two hands means one to eat, one to take pictures with!

10:30AM SOHO

SoHo is a trendy neighborhood that means South of Houston Street. Houston is pronounced how-sten you tourist. The original Baked by Melissas location is here, and tons of shopping including a Bloomingdales location.

11:30AM WASHINGTON SQUARE PARK

Most buildings around here are part of NYU, so this area is yung n cool. The park is popular for tourists and residents. Also, it has an arch, because what upstanding city wouldn't have an arch like this!

Tbh why does this arch exist in every city

1PM UNION SQUARE

Union Square is a little north of Washington Square Park and another popular destination. There's a farmers market here several days a week, and a Dylan's Candy Bar location. The best snack, though, is Chloe's Soft Serve Fruit Co. - an amazing fro yo place where the yogurt is made of fruit and ice.

2:30PM LEVAIN BAKERY

Home of the most insta-famous cookie of all time. Levain cookies are gigantic scones with gooey insides. The classic is chocolate chip walnut, but the double chocolate peanut butter is a close runner up. Lines can be long but you can think up your caption while you wait!

What dreams and love handles are made of

3:30PM SHEEP MEADOW

Sheep Meadow is a grassy field in Central Park. It has a great view of the NYC skyline and plenty of lawn space for picnics, sunbathing, frisbee, whatever your heart desires. Bring your Levain here and enjoy.

7:30PM THE SMITH

A restaurant that is both basic and great. Make sure you get the mac n cheese and the smores jar dessert. Also, pro tip: there's a photobooth downstairs by the bathroom.

10:30PM 230 FIFTH

230 Fifth is a huge rooftop bar in midtown. The roof is covered in palm trees... a lil tropical paradise in the concrete jungle. The views of the Empire State Building, which is lit up different colors each night, are great.

It's lit

12AM CLUBS IN MEATPACKING

Meatpacking is the trendiest area to go out. Bounce between 1 Oak, PH-D, and Le Bain at the Standard.




















sunday

10AM TOMPKINS SQUARE BAGELS

You can't leave NY without eating a bagel. Tompkins is famous for their crazy creations, like this birthday cake cream cheese on a tie-dye rainbow bagel. Go cray, or stick with the classics - either way, bagels everywhere else will never taste the same again.

Bae goals

11:30AM SMORGASBURG

Smorgasburg is the perfect last stop for your NYC visit. It takes place in Brooklyn Bridge Park on Sundays and has tons of food stands and picnic bench seating. Some of the most famous foods are the ramen burger, the lobster roll, the corn on a cob, and the ice cream sandwiches. Ca$h only so be prepared.

The hype is real
