WEEKEND ITINERARY

12PM BOHEMIA BAGELS

Welcome to Prague where their traditional food is so funky you don’t even need to feel guilty about eating other cuisines all weekend. This bagel chain is an American’s oasis in a strange, strange land. They have iced coffee too. #blessed

1PM THE JOHN LENNON WALL

Probably the biggest reason you’re visiting Prague am I right. Make sure you leave plenty of time to take thousands of options for your future profile picture! Lennon wall for likes!

Fisheye lens because fishing for likes, obv

2PM CHARLES BRIDGE

Famous, historic bridge. There are great views of both sides of the city from it. If it’s nice out, you can take a rowboat onto the river. Chances are it will be freezing though.

4PM OLD TOWN SQUARE

This is the heart of touristy Prague, and it looks straight outta Disney. Buy some hot wine or a pastry called a trdelnik from the street vendors - very worth it, even if you can’t pronounce it. The biggest sight here is the Astronomical Clock Tower, which doesn’t actually do anything cool. It’s literally just an old clock. But you can climb to the top of it and get a view of the whole square.

Throw your trdleniks in the air like you just don’t care (how to pronounce them)

7:30PM LA BODEGUITA

Pregame an epic night at this fun Cuban American restaurant. They have big drinks, salads, wings, sandwiches, etc. Trust me, you’ll be happier eating this than goulash and dumplings.

12AM LUCERNA

Lucerna Fridays are a magical thing. The club only plays 80s and 90s music, and American kids and Praha natives alike love it. If you get a ticket during the day, you can show up at 1AM, but if not get there are 12. When you finally stumble out at 4AM, you must get fried cheese from a street cart.

saturday

10:30AM WENCESLAS SQUARE

If you have any interest whatsoever in the history of the Czech Republic, this is for you. There’s a statue in this square to commemorate some sort of rebellion. Also, the national museum is here if you want to check that out.

12PM BAKESHOP

An incredible brunch spot. They have great prepared foods, baked goods, smoothies, etc. Very necessary for bringing you back to life post-Lucerna.

Wake and bake(shop). Czech.

2PM CASTLE DISTRICT

Walk back across the Charles Bridge and head up the hill towards the castle district. You can walk or take a tram. There’s a bunch of pretty old buildings and churches, and good views of the city. Don’t miss the changing of the guard ceremony - grab another trdelnik for the show.

5PM THAI MASSAGES

It’s been a long hungover day, you deserve a massage. There are little Thai massage places everywhere in Prague. Getting a massage is cheap, a hilarious experience, and obviously feels great. Treat yo self.

7:30PM CAFE MISTRAL

An all around great restaurant for dinner, as long as you reserve a table in the non-smoking section. We ate three course meals and drank for so cheap. You rock, Prague.

10:30PM KARLOVY LAZNE

A crazy, five story club. There’s also an ice bar here. You pay an entrance fee, and then they give you a coat and gloves and you go inside, and everything is made of ice - the tables, chairs, glasses - it’s so cool. Literally.

sunday

10AM CAFE SAVOY

This brunch spot has equally pretty atmosphere and food. Everything is amazing - get the full breakfast with orange juice, eggs, and fresh breads and croissants.

12PM JEWISH QUARTER

There are a bunch of synagogues you can wander in to, as well as a museum and a cemetery. It’s worthwhile to see and learn some history, if not just a pretty neighborhood to walk around in.
