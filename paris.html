PARIS, FRANCE

where the shopping is great and the food is even better.

WEEKEND ITINERARY

12PM LE MARAIS

The Jewish quarter of Paris has great shopping and even better food - dream come true. Walk around the streets by the St. Paul metro stop and hit stores like Cos, Allison, and Zadig & Voltaire. When you’re ready for lunch, brave the lines at L’As Du Fallafel - settling for any other falafel place is unacceptable. My friend who doesn’t even like falafel was obsessed. Don’t leave Le Marais without some chocolate rugelach from any of the hundred bakeries.

2PM NOTRE DAME

A famous church, surprise surprise. Admire its gothic architecture but don’t pay for the audio guide - you can walk through it for free. Also, don’t bother climbing it. There are better things to climb in Paris.

3PM PONT DES ARTS

Also known as the love lock bridge. Lovers write their names on a lock, attach it to this bridge, and throw the key into the River Seine below...gag. Hundreds of thousands of locks are attached to the bridge - sucks for the Parisian government, but a great insta opportunity for you.

4PM EIFFEL TOWER

Metro to La Dame de Fer, the really fucking weird name that French people use for the Eiffel Tower. If you get off at the École Militaire stop, you can walk through the park to the Eiffel Tower. Watch out for locals selling tourist trinkets and street food. The best photo op will be at Trocadéro, where you can fit the entire tower into your shot.

7:30PM SAINT-GERMAIN

Saint-Germain-des-Prés is a trendy arrondissement, or neighborhood. If you’re feeling dumb and rich, you can eat in one of the famous cafes where the existentialist movement was born - Les Deux Magots or Café de Flore. Otherwise, find a traditional brasserie and order some steak frites, coq au vin, french onion soup, and escargot (just so you can say you did it, obv)

10PM LATIN QUARTER

From Saint-Germain, it’s a short walk into the Latin Quarter. This area is the closest thing Paris has to a collegetown, because it’s home to the Sorbonne. Any bar or club in this area will be fun, but Chez Georges has a wine cave, so it wins. End your night with a crêpe from City Crêpes. If you don’t eat a crêpe for each day you’re in Paris, you’re doing it wrong.
