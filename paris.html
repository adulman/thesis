WEEKEND ITINERARY

friday

12PM LE MARAIS

The Jewish quarter of Paris has great shopping and even better food - dream come true. Walk around the streets by the St. Paul metro stop and hit stores like Cos, Allison, and Zadig & Voltaire. When you’re ready for lunch, brave the lines at L’As Du Fallafel - settling for any other falafel place is unacceptable. My friend who doesn’t even like falafel was obsessed. Don’t leave Le Marais without some chocolate rugelach from any of the hundred bakeries.

2PM NOTRE DAME

A famous church, surprise surprise. Admire its gothic architecture but don’t pay for the audio guide - you can walk through it for free. Also, don’t bother climbing it. There are better things to climb in Paris.

3PM PONT DES ARTS

Also known as the love lock bridge. Lovers write their names on a lock, attach it to this bridge, and throw the key into the River Seine below...gag. Hundreds of thousands of locks are attached to the bridge - sucks for the Parisian government, but a great insta opportunity for you.

Sucks for the Parisian government, but a great insta opportunity for you

4PM EIFFEL TOWER

Metro to La Dame de Fer, the really fucking weird name that French people use for the Eiffel Tower. If you get off at the Ecole Militaire stop, you can walk through the park to the Eiffel Tower. Watch out for locals selling tourist trinkets and street food. The best photo op will be at Trocadero, where you can fit the entire tower into your shot.

7:30PM SAINT-GERMAIN

Saint-Germain-des-Pres is a trendy arrondissement, or neighborhood. If you’re feeling dumb and rich, you can eat in one of the famous cafes where the existentialist movement was born - Les Deux Magots or Cafe de Flore. Otherwise, find a traditional brasserie and order some steak frites, coq au vin, french onion soup, and escargot (just so you can say you did it, obv)

10PM LATIN QUARTER

From Saint-Germain, it’s a short walk into the Latin Quarter. This area is the closest thing Paris has to a collegetown, because it’s home to the Sorbonne. Any bar or club in this area will be fun, but Chez Georges has a wine cave, so it wins. End your night with a crepe from City Crepes. If you don’t eat a crepe for each day you’re in Paris, you’re doing it wrong.

saturday

9:30AM CAFE ANGELINA

Early wake up call, sorry, but the hot chocolate and pastries at Cafe Angelina will be worth it. It’s a little pricey but you’ll feel like Eloise in the fucking Plaza, pardon my French.

10:30AM THE LOUVRE

Visiting Paris without seeing the Mona Lisa is rude. Yes, she’s a little unimpressive in person, but you can still tell your Mom you saw her. Also, the Louvre is huge and full of other famous shit, so you can walk off your breakfast while getting cultured. Win win.

Visiting paris without seeing the Mona Lisa is rude

12PM CHAMPS-ELYSEES

When you exit the Louvre, walk through the Tuileries gardens and they’ll lead you to the 5th Avenue of France: The Champs-Elysees. Shop til you drop (every dollar to your name) at Hermes, Louis Vuitton, Longchamp, Chanel, Goyard, Van Cleef & Arpels, and every other top French brand. Careful not to step on the lines of Asians. If you’re hungry, grab some macarons from Laduree or a baguette sandwich from Paul.

2PM ARC DE TRIOMPHE

At the end of the Champs, that giant arch is the Arc de Triomphe. I think it’s pretty important and historical in some way. More importantly it’s also the best view in Paris. Climb it and you’ll see all of Paris, including the Eiffel Tower. Hopefully the 200+ stairs will help you say au revoir to all the baguettes and crepes you’ve been eating.

4PM MONTMARTRE & SACRE COEUR

Metro to the Montmartre area of Paris. You can take the cable car up the hill from the Abesses metro stop, or climb the stairs (beware - it’s a lot). At the top, there’s a square full of artists painting portraits and selling their work. It’s really, really cute. Sacre Coeur, another famous church, is here too. Grab a bottle of wine and watch the sun set over Paris from the hill in front of it.

7PM LE REFUGE DES FONDUS

You’re already drunk so might as well keep it going. This hole-in-the-wall restaurant in Montmartre is an abroadee favorite. Stuff your face with fondue and drink wine out of a baby bottle, because wine not? Don’t leave without signing your name on the wall.

Wine not drink out of a baby bottle?

10PM SHOWCASE & SOCIAL CLUB

No judgement if you feel the need to pass out at this point, but if you’re up for it, head to Showcase for a true European clubbing experience. End your night at Social Club. Both are crowded, sweaty, and hold the promise of amazing stories, if you don’t lose your phone.




















sunday

10AM VERSAILLES

Marie Antoinette’s old crib is only a quick train ride outside the city. Catch the RER C to Versailles Rive Gauche, pick up a free audio tape, and you’ll be taking the ultimate selfie in the Hall of Mirrors before you know it. If it’s nice out, leave plenty of time to explore the massive gardens too.
