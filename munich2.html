MUNICH, GERMANY

where beer is the new water

WEEKEND ITINERARY

10AM ENGLISH GARDEN

Chinese tower We did a 3 hour bike tour of the city again through Viator for 24 euros Shows you a lot of the main sights You also get to stop at the beer garden at the Chinese Tower in the English Gardens which is a to-do thing in Munich

Chinese Tower in the English Gardens in Germany, ok ok

10:30AM HAUPTBAHNHOF

central train station We ate in the train station Hauptbahnhof like every day lol It’s really nice inside and soooo many food options - every cuisine - Asian, Mexican, breakfast food, etc. Coffee Fellows and Asia Hung were both good, and the Chupitas smoothie stand

11:30AM OKTOBERFEST / SPRINGFEST

Theresienwiese. OR day trip to Neuschwanstein or Dachau concentration camp. SPRINGFEST This is the spring version of Oktoberfest - happens for 3 weekends in April but most people I know went the same weekend I did, 4/25 It’s a huge darty basically - a beer festival AND a carnival and soooo much fun You dress up, drink a lot, socialize with abroad kids, ride rides, play games, eat carnival food or beer hall food - best day ever I HIGHLY recommend going Get there early to get a table inside the beer hall - before noon. There’s only 2 beer halls. If you go for Oktoberfest I heard you have to get there at like 7am Bring your license (you have to show ID) Order beer with lemonade Tip your waitress each round so she keeps coming back to you! OUTFITS Underratedly SO EXPENSIVE. The dresses are like 60 minimum but more like 100-120 at most places near Hauptbahnhof. Go to Street Wear near Jaeger’s - can haggle the guy down to 50 euros for a dress Apparently they’re cheaper in the city center / if you go to a mall but we didn’t really investigate Eating at Springfest - giant pretzels, bratwursts, fries, etc
