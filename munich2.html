MUNICH, GERMANY

where beer is the new water

WEEKEND ITINERARY

10AM ENGLISH GARDEN

The English Gardens are a huge park in Munich. You can do a bike tour through them or explore on your own. The Chinese Tower is one of the most famous sights inside, and it's another beer garden, shocker!! But the pretzels are bomb so you should stop and grab one.

Chinese Tower in the English Gardens in Germany, ok ok

10:30AM HAUPTBAHNHOF

Hauptbahnhof is the central train station and it's legit nice. There are tons of food options and we underratedly ate here like every day because we were always passing through. Coffee Fellows, Asia Hung, and the Chupitas smoothie stand are all on point.

11:30AM OKTOBERFEST / SPRINGFEST

I'm assuming you're in Munich for either Oktoberfest or Springfest but if you're really weird and there on a random weekend, you can do a day trip to Dachau concentration camp or Neuschwanstein castle - Google it. If you are here for Oktober or Springfest, YES great decision. Get ready for the time of your life - it's a huge darty basically that's half beer festival and half carnival. There are tons of rides and food and fun costumes - dressing up is a must. The festival grounds are Theresienwiese and there are a bunch of different beer halls. Get there early to get a table, and keep tipping your waitress so she comes back. It is wiiiild inside but you can alternate going on the rides and chugging beers. For food, get the giant pretzels, bratwursts, fries, and cotton candy. Number one thing to remember: "prost" is "cheers" in German. Prost to that.
