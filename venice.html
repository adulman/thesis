WEEKEND ITINERARY

friday

12PM ST. MARK'S SQUARE

One of the main sights of Venice. The church St. Mark’s Basilica is famous, and the Doge’s Palace is here too, whatever that is. This square is known for its pigeons (ew) and the fact that it floods frequently. Welcome to Venice.

2PM SHOPPING

Walk the streets near St. Mark’s square and wander into the touristy shops. Venice is famous for its Venetian glass and masks. If it’s Carnevale season, pick up a mask and strap it to your face for the rest of the weekend. Everything is more fun in masks.

know too many people abroad? hide with a mask!

5PM RIALTO BRIDGE

This bridge is in the center of Venice. It has beautiful views and thousands of tourists at all times. If you have noticed yet, Venice is a lot like DisneyLand.

7PM TAVERNA SAN LIO

This place has the best seafood in all of Venice. It’s a close walk from Rialto, and you won’t be disappointed.

saturday

10:30AM GUGGENHEIM MUSEUM

Like the one in New York, except better, because you’re in Venice. It’s located right on the Grand Canal and has modern art from all the top names: Picasso, Magritte, Pollock, Brancusi, and more.

12PM DAL MORO’S FRESH PASTA TO GO

A great lunch place where you customize a fresh pasta and sauce order. It comes in a takeout container, and you can go chill on the nearest bridge to enjoy it. Don’t be surprised if you end up wanting to eat here for dinner, too.

pasta with a view #blessed

2PM GONDOLA RIDE

Try to find a gondolier on a side street so that it won’t be as expensive as the touristy areas, but either way, this is a must-do in Venice. The standard ride is 30 minutes, and yes, the gondoliers really do sing.

boats n' hoes

3PM BURANO

Take a ferry to Burano, a nearby island with rainbow houses along the water. Unclear if people actually live here but it’s truly gorgeous.

7:30PM ROSSOPOMODORO

When you’re back in Venice, get some great pizza from Rossopomodoro. This chain is in every major Italian city and is consistently on point. In pizza we crust.




















sunday

10AM JEWISH QUARTER

You can walk around on your own, or do the Jewish Ghetto and Museum tour for 7 euros. This area of Venice has cute parks and stores!

12PM GAM GAM

Grab lunch at this spot in the Jewish Quarter before saying ciao to Venice. They have amazing matzo ball soup and potato latkes. Get your nosh on.
