BUDAPEST, HUNGARY

where everything is weird but cool

WEEKEND ITINERARY

10AM STIKA

text

stika

11AM ST. STEPHEN'S BASILICA

Big impressive church in a very cute / central area of Budapest Go to the top! Stairs or elevator - same price (pretty cheap) and the view is great up there

basilica

12:30PM HUNGARIAN PARLIAMENT TOUR

Book this online in advance Takes about an hour The building is incredible - so ornate and beautiful. Definitely worth the tour

1:30PM SHOES ON THE DANUBE

Holocaust Memorial near Chain Bridge / Parliament Just takes a walk by to see it

2PM CHAIN BRIDGE

The most famous bridge in Budapest Views of both sides of the river bank Go at night and during the day

chain bridge

2:30PM CENTRAL MARKET HALL

Closed Sundays Good for lunch or a snack or just to walk around and see. The bottom floor is groceries more, the top floor more food stands, and also a huge flea market Get lango’s - they’re like fried bread frisbees that they top with either sweet stuff like nutella / cinnamon sugar, etc, or chicken, cheese, sour cream, sausage, etc We got beef goulash in bread bowls - pretty good Also try kreme’s - traditional Hungarian cakes

market

8:00PM GELLERT THERMAL BATHS

Different famous baths in the city We found these to be a lot nicer than the Szechenyi ones, and it was the same price They’re at the Gellert Hotel, and much smaller, but more beautiful You can get massages here too just call to book because the schedules get full

8:00PM GELLERT HILL

liberty statue. hi We didn’t end up having time to climb this but it’s a huge hill people climb for a good view of the whole city Liberty monument at the top

gellert hill

8:00PM TG Italiano

Tom George - good Italian food

8:00PM SZIMPLA KERT

The most famous ruins bar in Budapest Ruins bars are everywhere and so cool - they’re like junkyards turned into really cool, edgy, eclectic, chill bars Szimpla Kert has a mix of visitors and locals and is partially inside partially outside and SO COOL and drinks are beyond cheap

szimpla
